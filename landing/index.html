Current framing

What it is

Pixelbox is the native shell for coding with agents, terminals, and live previews.

Where it came from

It started inside the Pixel Claw idea space and is now becoming its own product line.

What happens next

This landing page becomes the public heartbeat: changelog updates, milestones, screenshots, and product notes.

Why it exists

The gap between “write code” and “see behavior” is still too wide.

Most local workflows spread context across a terminal, a browser, a chat pane, and whatever tab still has the last useful error. Pixelbox collapses that into one place and treats agent work like a first-class local runtime.

Core loop

Switch. Run. Observe. Edit.

Project-aware sessions, embedded preview, and terminal continuity make the loop harder to drop.

Positioning

Not just a launcher

Pixelbox is the workspace layer around AI software creation, not another terminal skin or static app shell.
